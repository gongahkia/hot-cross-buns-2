Hot Cross Buns

Privacy and trust

What Hot Cross Buns stores, requests, and does not send.

Hot Cross Buns is a Mac app that talks directly to Google Tasks and Google Calendar. There is no separate Hot Cross Buns backend.

Google access

Hot Cross Buns asks Google only for the scopes it needs to read and write your selected task lists and calendars:

  • https://www.googleapis.com/auth/tasks
  • https://www.googleapis.com/auth/calendar

The Tasks scope lets the app list task lists, read tasks, create tasks, update task titles, notes, due dates, completion state, hierarchy, and delete tasks or task lists when you choose those actions. The Calendar scope lets the app list calendars, read events, create events, update event details, reminders, recurrence, attendees, location, Google Meet links, and delete events when you choose those actions. Hot Cross Buns does not request Drive, Gmail, Contacts, or Photos access.

How Google data is used

Google user data is used only to provide the visible task, note, calendar, search, reminder, template, cleanup, export, and sync features in the Mac app. The app sends your edits directly to Google Tasks and Google Calendar for the currently active account, reads Google responses back into the local cache, and uses local copies for fast search, offline display, pending offline writes, diagnostics, and optional local notifications.

Hot Cross Buns does not sell Google user data, use it for advertising, transfer it to data brokers, or use it to train AI models.

Where local data lives

By default the app keeps a local cache under ~/Library/Application Support/com.gongahkia.hotcrossbuns.mac/.

  • cache-state.sqlite: account metadata, active-account state, per-account settings, sync checkpoints, pending offline mutations, task mirrors, calendar mirrors, and event mirrors
  • cache-state.json and cache-events.json: legacy cache sidecars that are migrated into SQLite on first load and used only as fallback inputs
  • cache-state.salt: encryption salt when cache encryption is enabled
  • crash.log: local crash note written on certain fatal crashes

The cache is replaceable. Google remains the source of truth for synced tasks and events. When multiple Google accounts are connected, each account has its own cached workspace and only the active account is synced or replayed.

Encryption and credentials

Google OAuth tokens stay in the macOS Keychain and are used to request fresh access tokens from Google when the app syncs. For custom Desktop OAuth, the OAuth client configuration is stored once for the app, while token sets are stored separately by Google account ID. Optional local cache encryption uses AES-256-GCM with a passphrase-derived key. That encryption protects local cache files on your Mac; it does not change what Google stores.

Sharing and retention

There is no Hot Cross Buns server, hosted database, or analytics backend receiving your Google Tasks or Google Calendar data. Data is shared with Google because Google is the storage and sync provider you connect to. Calendar events may also be visible to event attendees or calendar subscribers according to your Google Calendar settings.

Disconnecting a Google account in the app removes that account's local tokens and workspace on this Mac. If other accounts remain connected, the app can switch to another account; disconnecting the last account leaves the app signed out locally. You can also revoke the app's access from your Google Account. Data already stored in Google Tasks or Google Calendar remains in your Google account unless you delete it there or delete it through Hot Cross Buns.

Crash logs and telemetry

Hot Cross Buns does not include a third-party analytics or crash-upload SDK. macOS may still produce system crash reports in your Library logs, and the app may write a local crash note next to the cache to help diagnose launch failures. Those files stay on-device unless you choose to share them.

Notes, tasks, and events

Tasks and calendar events are backed by Google Tasks and Google Calendar. Notes in the app are task-shaped items that use Google Tasks storage, typically by keeping the note body in the Google Tasks notes field and leaving the due date empty.

Permissions

Notifications are optional and used only for device-local reminders from the active workspace. Accessibility permission is only needed if you enable global hotkeys that should work outside the app. You can revoke Google account access from your Google account settings and disconnect Hot Cross Buns from the app’s Settings window.

OAuth client ownership

Hot Cross Buns is intended to use a Google Cloud OAuth client controlled by the person or organization using the app. Downloaded DMGs can store one user-supplied Desktop OAuth client in the macOS Keychain and use a localhost browser redirect for each Google account sign-in. Source builds can also embed a native Google Sign-In client at build time. Do not use another person's OAuth client unless you trust that build and consent screen.

Release integrity

Each GitHub release publishes a matching SHA-256 checksum file for the DMG. The install script downloads both the DMG and checksum, verifies them locally, and refuses to install if they do not match.

Distribution model

Preview DMGs are currently distributed outside the Mac App Store. They may be unsigned or not notarized, so macOS can require a one-time manual approval under System Settings > Privacy & Security. Hot Cross Buns can check GitHub Releases for a newer DMG, but installation is still a manual replace rather than a silent in-place update.
